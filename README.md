# Secrets of the Copper Still

Description:

Secrets of the Copper Still is a mobile application for collectors of vintage chemical distillation apparatus, historians of early pharmacology, and preservers of industrial laboratory heritage, dedicated to cataloging the non-alcoholic essential oil extractors, steam-jacketed copper alembics, and analog fractional columns that isolated perfumes, botanical medicines, and pure industrial solvents before the era of digital laboratory automation and synthetic chemical synthesis.
Systematize your collection of the vessels of extraction—from hand-hammered copper alembic heads with goose-neck condensers used to process floral waters to heavy laboratory glass retorts, from manual gravity-fed separation funnels to early brass hydrometers calibrated for specific gravity testing in industrial perfume labs.
Create a digital archive of heavy industry by recording the instrument type, manufacturer, temperature range, era, and the specific steel mill, foundry, or ceramic kiln each specimen once calibrated.

## Bespoke Object Fields (Model "Distillation Vessel"):

•	Alembic Ledger Key: A unique, system-generated alphanumeric laboratory asset string (e.g., SCS-STILL-2291-BOTAN-M).

•	Apparatus Classification: The technical category of the distillation component (e.g., Gooseneck Alembic, Worm Condenser, Fractional Column, Glass Retort, Separation Funnel).

•	Artisan Hallmark: Fictionalized coppersmiths and scientific laboratory glassworks (e.g., AetherAlembic Smithing, Meridian Scientific Glass, Providence Copper & Brass).

•	Condensation Surface Area: The geometric cooling parameters designed into the system (e.g., 120 square-inch jacketed surface, 8-turn copper spiral).

•	Volumetric Capacity Bounds: The precise liquid limit the boiling pot or collection vessel can manage (e.g., 5-Gallon Boiling Pot, 500mL Retort Flask).

•	Joint Joinery Architecture: The method used to seal the high-temperature chemical vapor paths (e.g., Ground-glass standard taper joint, riveted copper flange with linseed paste seal, threaded brass union).

•	Metallurgical Gauge Thickness: For metal vessels, the sheet metal density (e.g., 16-gauge hammered copper, 2mm borosilicate glass rating).

•	Physical Proportions: The absolute height from base to the crest of the neck, setup footprint size, and empty mass in kilograms.

•	Preservation Soundness: The physical state of the sealing pathways (e.g., Copper pitting depth, glass scoring lines, verdigris corrosion percentage).

•	Laboratory Ground Zero: The fictionalized municipal botanical laboratory, historic apothecary firm, or rosewater processing plant where the item was actively deployed (e.g., Forgotten alpine herbal distillery, historic coastal perfume lab warehouse, old city industrial chemical archives).
